Celestine Ukwu

Celestine Ukwu was a pioneering Igbo highlife musician of the 1960s and 1970s, known for his philosophical lyrics and timeless hits like Ije Enu and Igede, now preserved through subtitled videos and English translations.

02

Osita Osadebe

Chief Stephen Osita Osadebe, the King of Highlife, transformed traditional Igbo singing into modern highlife music, with over 500 songs including classics like Osondi Owendi, brought to life through archival footage and lyric translations.

Read More

03

Oliver De Coque

Oliver De Coque was a legendary Igbo highlife guitarist and singer, famous for tracks like People's Club of Nigeria, whose cultural stories and melodies are preserved in our subtitled videos and historical commentary.

Read More

04

Oriental Brothers

The Oriental Brothers, led by Dr. Sir Warrior, defined 1970s Igbo highlife with rootsy sounds and social commentary, now accessible to new generations via translated lyrics and rare archival performances.

Nelly Uchendu

Nelly Uchendu, the Lady with the Golden Voice, modernized Igbo folk highlife with songs like Love Nwantinti, preserved through our project's subtitled classics and cultural storytelling.

Top Hits from Our Star Highlife Legends

Ife Si na Chi - Celestine Ukwu

Celestine Ukwu's "Ife Si na Chi" is a profound highlife masterpiece, weaving Igbo philosophy into soulful melodies, now preserved with English subtitles to inspire new generations.

Osondi Owendi - Osita Osadebe

Osita Osadebe's "Osondi Owendi," meaning "one man's meat is another's poison," is a timeless highlife anthem, celebrated for its soothing rhythms and accessible through translated lyrics.

Watch Now
Ifeoma - Prince Emeka Morocco

Prince Emeka Morocco's "Ifeoma" is a vibrant highlife track that celebrates love and community, encouraging listeners to give back to society, preserved with subtitled videos and cultural insights.

Chi Awu Otu - Oriental Brothers

The Oriental Brothers' "Chi Awu Otu," led by Dr. Sir Warrior, is a 1970s highlife classic with deep social messages, brought to life through our project's archival footage and translations.
